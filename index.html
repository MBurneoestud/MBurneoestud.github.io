Ethics, Bias & Accountability in AI-Driven Finance

AI is increasingly used in finance for: credit scoring, algorithm trading, fraud detection, insurance pricing, and investment recommendations

Key Topics

Ethical Frameworks

AI in finance shifts power from human judgment to statistical prediction. Finance becomes less about intention and more about probability. The ethical challenge is not whether AI makes mistakes, but how societies govern decisions made by systems no individual fully controls.

Algorithmic Bias

Algorithms inherit historical discrimination, data quality determines fairness. Eg: if an institution historically favors wealthy borrowers, AI predicts wealthy borrowers as safer and can become socially unequal. This becomes especially problematic when considering minority groups.

Accountability

If an algorithm causes financial harm, who is responsible? The programmer, the financial institution, the regulators, the AI system itself?

What happens when financial decisions are made by algorithms instead of humans?

The implementation of AI in finance creates new ethical quandaries, introducing new and uncontrolled sources of bias, as well as obfuscating the origins of problems, thus disrupting accountability.
